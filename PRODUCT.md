# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Instagram visitors, first. People tap through from a Reel where Shrivas builds this site on camera — designers, potential ENTØ clients, other founders, friends. They arrive on a phone, curious, with a 10-second attention fuse. The site must hook them in the first screen and reward every tap after it.

Second audience: anyone who googles "Shrivas VM" — collaborators, university people, future clients. They stay longer and read the story fragments.

## Product Purpose

A personal brand statement built as a playable machine: SHRIVAS-64, a fictional Japanese retro game console OS. The visitor boots it, lands on a living home screen (wallpaper: a messy room), and explores cartridge widgets that each hold a fragment of who Shrivas is — the story (Coimbatore → Netherlands, founder at ENTØ), what he builds, what he plays and listens to, how to reach him.

Success: the visitor screenshots it, shares it, or says "how was this made?" It is not a resume. It proves craft by being craft.

## Brand Personality

Mischievous · cinematic · precise.

The voice of a machine with a personality: system messages that tease ("note to self: try clicking around"), boot text played straight, dialog boxes that talk back. Film-grade art direction — grain, phosphor glow, deliberate framing. And under all the play, pixel-exact execution: aligned grids, tuned motion curves, zero jank.

## Anti-references

- The template dev portfolio: hero + about + projects grid + contact form.
- Generic vaporwave: purple/pink synthwave gradients, palm trees, chrome text.
- Corporate minimal: safe white space, Inter, muted grays. A SaaS page about a person.
- Web3 slop: neon glow + glassmorphism cards + gradient orbs on dark.
- Any layout a visitor has already scrolled past twice today.

## Design Principles

1. **It's a machine, not a page.** Every surface behaves like part of the console: boots, clicks, hums. Navigation is exploration, never a scroll.
2. **Reward curiosity.** Every clickable thing does something. Hidden details exist for the person who pokes around longest.
3. **The fiction never breaks.** Loading states, errors, and settings all speak in the console's voice. No generic UI leaks through.
4. **Every screen is a poster.** Any freeze-frame must survive being screenshotted and posted. Composition, texture, and type are judged frame by frame.
5. **Play on the surface, discipline underneath.** Grain and chaos are art-directed; contrast, touch targets, reduced-motion, and performance are non-negotiable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA: body text ≥ 4.5:1 against its actual backdrop (grain and wallpaper included), large display type ≥ 3:1.
- Full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative: crossfades instead of CRT flicker/boot choreography; no autoplaying shake or strobe.
- CRT scanline/flicker effects stay subtle — no strobing above 3 flashes/second, ever.
- Keyboard: every widget openable and closable without a mouse; visible focus states styled in-fiction (pixel selection brackets).
- Touch targets ≥ 44×44px; the console is fully playable one-thumbed on a 375px phone.
- Audio is opt-in at boot (default respects the visitor), mutable everywhere.
